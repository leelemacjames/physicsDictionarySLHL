Invariant[dimensionless]HL EXT
A quantity that has the same value for all observers under a specified transformation (e.g., the space–time interval is invariant under Lorentz transformations).
Builds on SL: Master glossary.
SymmetryHL EXT
A property of a system or law that remains unchanged under a transformation (translation, rotation, time shift). In physics, symmetries often imply conservation laws.
Builds on SL: Master glossary.
LinearisationHL EXT
Rewriting a relationship into a straight‑line form (e.g., log–log or semi‑log) so gradients/intercepts can be used to determine constants and exponents.
Builds on SL: Master glossary.
Validity DomainHL EXT
The range of conditions in which a model or equation is expected to hold (e.g., Galilean velocity addition is valid when v ≪ c).
Builds on SL: Master glossary.
Limiting CaseHL EXT
A simplified extreme of a model (e.g., small‑angle approximation; or relativistic equations reducing to Newtonian forms when v ≪ c) used to check consistency.
Builds on SL: Master glossary.

The mean value predicted by a probability distribution. In quantum contexts, it is the average outcome of many measurements of an observable.

A
Absorbed Dose (D) D [Gy or J kg⁻¹] HL
Total energy absorbed per unit mass of tissue.
Acoustic Impedance (Z) Z [kg m⁻² s⁻¹] HL
The product of the density of a substance and the speed of sound in that substance.
Angular Acceleration α [rad s⁻²] HL
The rate of change of angular velocity. Analogous to linear acceleration in rotational motion.
See also: Angular Speed, Torque.
Angular Momentum L [kg m² s⁻¹] HL
The rotational equivalent of linear momentum. For a rotating object, it is the product of moment of inertia and angular velocity (L = Iω).
See also: Angular Speed, Torque, Conservation of Angular Momentum.
B
Biological Half-life (TB) TB [s] HL
Time taken for half the number of ingested radioactive nuclei in the body to be removed by natural bodily (chemical) processes.
Black Holes HL
A region of spacetime with extreme curvature due to the presence of a mass.
C
Carbon-Oxygen Burning HL
Advanced nuclear fusion stage in massive stars where carbon and oxygen nuclei fuse to create heavier elements like neon, magnesium, and silicon.
See also: Helium Burning, Nuclear Fusion.
Center of a Black Hole (singularity) HL
The single point to which all mass would collapse.
Conservation of Angular Momentum HL
In the absence of external torques, the total angular momentum of a system remains constant.
See also: Angular Momentum, Torque.
Couple [N m] HL
A pair of equal and opposite parallel forces that tend to cause rotation only. The net force is zero, but the net torque is non-zero.
See also: Torque, Turning Moment, Force.
Critical Density ρc [kg m⁻³] HL
The average density of matter in the universe required for it to eventually stop expanding and begin to contract. Determines the ultimate fate of the universe.
D
Davisson-Germer Experiment HL
An experiment showing that electrons are scattered off crystals of nickel and interfere with each other – also known as 'electron diffraction.' This experiment is evidence for the existence of matter waves.
Dose Equivalent (H) H [Sv] HL
Product of quality factor and absorbed dose which is an attempt to measure the radiation damage that actually occurs in tissues.
E
Effective Half-life (TE) TE [s] HL
Time taken for the number of radioactive nuclei present in the body to halve.
Electron in a Box Model HL
A model of the atom useful for explaining the origin of atomic energy levels: The model assumes that, if an electron is confined to move in one dimension by a box, the de Broglie waves associated with the electron will be standing waves of wavelength 2L/n where L is the length of the box and n is a positive integer. Further, the kinetic energy of the electron in the box is n²h²/(8meL²).
Exposure (X) X [C kg⁻¹] HL
Measure of the total amount of ionization produced which is the ratio of total charge to mass of air.
G
Galilean Transformation HL
Equations that translate measurements made in one frame of reference to another frame of reference without taking into account the theory of relativity.
Gravitational Mass mg [kg] HL
The property of an object that determines how much gravitational force it feels when near another object. Different gravitational masses have different gravitational forces acting between them.
Gravitational Red Shift HL
A prediction of the general theory relativity in which clocks slow down in a gravitational field (that is, clocks on the ground floor of a building will run slowly as compared with clocks on the top floor).
H
Hafele-Keating Experiment HL
A 1971 test of the predictions of time dilation in which atomic clocks were put in aircraft and flown, east and west, around the world and then compared with clocks that remained fixed in the same location on Earth.
Half-Value Thickness (x₁/₂) x₁/₂ [m] HL
The thickness of a material needed for a beam (of X-rays) to be attenuated (reduced) in intensity by 50%.
Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle HL
Conjugate quantities (position-momentum or time-energy) cannot be known precisely at the same time. There is a link between the uncertainty principle and the de Broglie hypothesis.
Helium Burning HL
Nuclear fusion process in which helium nuclei combine to form heavier elements, primarily carbon. Occurs in the core of stars after hydrogen is exhausted.
See also: Nuclear Fusion, Red Giant, Carbon-Oxygen Burning.
Hertzsprung-Russell Diagram H-R Diagram HL
A scatter plot of stars showing the relationship between their absolute magnitudes (or luminosities) and spectral types (or temperatures). Used to understand stellar evolution.
See also: Luminosity, Absolute Magnitude, Red Giant, White Dwarf, Blue Giant.
I
Inertial Frame of Reference HL
A frame of reference that is not accelerating but is at rest or moving with a constant velocity. OR A frame of reference in which Newton's law of inertia is valid.
Inertial Mass mi [kg] HL
The property of an object that determines how much it accelerates when a given force is applied to it. OR The ratio of resultant force to acceleration of an object.
Inertial Mass-Energy E [J] HL
The total energy content of an object including its rest mass energy and kinetic energy, as described by Einstein's mass-energy equivalence.
See also: Rest Mass, Kinetic Energy (EK), Special Theory of Relativity.
L
Length Contraction HL
An effect of relativity in which the separation between two points in space contracts if there is relative motion in that direction.
See also: Special Theory of Relativity, Proper Length, Lorentz Factor (γ).
Light Clock HL
A beam of light reflected between two parallel mirrors used to measure time.
Lorentz Factor (γ) γ [dimensionless] HL
A relativistic factor that varies from approximately 1 at low velocities to approaching infinity near the speed of light.
See also: Special Theory of Relativity, Time Dilation, Length Contraction.
M
Matter Waves HL
All moving particles have a 'matter wave' associated with them whose wavelength is the de Broglie wavelength.
See also: de Broglie Hypothesis, Wave-Particle Duality, Electron.
Michelson-Morley Experiment HL
An experiment comparing the speed of light measured in two perpendicular directions using interference patterns. Did not produce any observable difference in the speeds – implies that the ether does not exist and that the result is consistent with the constancy of the speed of light.
Millikan's Stopping Potential Experiment HL
An experiment utilizing reverse voltage raised to such a level (stopping potential Vs) that it stops all emitted photoelectrons. This experiment is used to test the Einstein model of the explaining the photoelectric effect.
Muon Decay Experiment HL
An experiment comparing the number of muons in cosmic rays that reach the Earth's surface without decaying as compared to the number predicted to hit the surface calculated without using relativity theory. The results give evidence to support the special theory of relativity.
O
Optically Active Substance HL
One that rotates the plane of polarization of the light that passes through it. OR One that changes the plane in which the electric field vector of the light vibrates.
P
Pion Decay Experiment HL
An experiment involving the decay of a fast-moving pion into two gamma ray photons – indicates that the speed of light in a vacuum is independent of its source.
Pixel HL
Small area of a CCD that acts as a capacitor.
Principle of Equivalence HL
A postulate that states there is no difference between an accelerating frame of reference and a gravitational field.
Proper Length L₀ [m] HL
The length of an object recorded in a frame of reference where the object is at rest. This is the greatest possible length that could be recorded for an object.
Proper Time Interval Δt₀ [s] HL
The time between events as measured in a frame where the events take place at the same point in space. This is the shortest possible time that any observer could correctly record for the event.
R
Radiation Pressure P [Pa] HL
The pressure exerted on a surface by electromagnetic radiation. Due to the momentum carried by photons.
See also: Electromagnetic Radiation, Photon, Force.
Rayleigh Criterion HL
When the central maximum of one diffraction pattern overlaps the first minimum of a second diffraction pattern, the two sources are 'just resolved.'
Rest Mass m₀ [kg] HL
The mass of an object when it is at rest relative to an observer. An invariant quantity in special relativity.
See also: Inertial Mass.
S
Schrödinger Model of the Atom HL
This model assumes that electrons in the atom may be described by wavefunctions. The electron has an undefined position, but the square of the amplitude of the wavefunction gives the probability of finding the electron at a particular point.
Schwarzschild Radius (RS) RS [m] HL
A particular distance from the center of black hole where the escape velocity is equal to the speed of light.
Simultaneity HL
Two events occurring at different points in space and which are simultaneous for one observer cannot be simultaneous for another observer in a different frame of reference.
Sound Intensity I [W m⁻²] HL
Amount of energy that a sound wave brings to a unit area every second. OR Power incident per unit area. Intensity depends on the square of the amplitude of the sound wave.
Sound Intensity Level (IL) IL [dB] HL
One tenth of the common logarithm of the ratio of the intensity of a sound to the sound intensity at the threshold of hearing (measured in decibels (dB)).
Spacetime HL
Four dimensional coordinates used to describe any event (three spatial dimensions and time). Moving objects follow the shortest path between two points in spacetime. Gravitational attraction can be explained by the warping of spacetime.
Special Theory of Relativity HL
Consists of two postulates: 1) The laws of physics are the same in all inertial reference frames. 2) The speed of light in a vacuum is the same for all observers.
See also: Time Dilation, Length Contraction, Lorentz Factor (γ), Spacetime.
Stokes Law HL
Describes the drag force on a spherical object moving through a viscous fluid at low Reynolds numbers. The drag force is proportional to velocity, fluid viscosity, and object radius.
See also: Laminar Flow.
Strong Force HL
The fundamental force responsible for binding quarks together to form protons and neutrons, and binding protons and neutrons together in atomic nuclei. Strongest of the four fundamental forces but very short range.
See also: Weak Decay, Coulomb Force.
Surface of a Black Hole (event horizon) HL
Where the escape speed is equal to c and within this surface, mass has 'disappeared' from the universe.
T
Time Dilation HL
An effect of relativity in which moving clocks run slow.
See also: Special Theory of Relativity, Proper Time Interval, Lorentz Factor (γ).
Torque τ [N m] HL
The rotational equivalent of force; the quantity that causes a change in angular momentum. The turning effect of a force about an axis or pivot point.
See also: Turning Moment, Force, Angular Acceleration, Couple.
Turning Moment [N m] HL
Another name for moment or torque. The product of a force and the perpendicular distance from the pivot to the line of action of the force.
See also: Torque, Force, Couple.
Twin Paradox HL
Different observers' measurements of the time taken for a journey at speeds close to the speed of light will not be the same. Since one of the twins has to accelerate in the spaceship, this is no longer a symmetrical situation for the twins.
W
Wave-Particle Duality HL
Both matter and radiation have a dual nature. They exhibit both particle and wave properties.
See also: Matter Waves, Photon, de Broglie Hypothesis.
Weak Decay HL
Nuclear decay process mediated by the weak nuclear force, such as beta decay. Responsible for processes that change one type of quark into another.
See also: Strong Force, Radioactive Decay.
D
de Broglie Hypothesis HL
All particles can behave like waves whose wavelength is given by λ = h/p where h is Planck's constant and p is the momentum of the particle.
See also: Matter Waves, Wavelength (λ), Linear Momentum (p), Wave-Particle Duality.
